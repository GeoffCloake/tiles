Game Rules

How to Play

Game Setup

The game can begin with starter tiles placed on the board, marked with grey circles. Each player receives a rack of tiles. Board size, rack size and starter tiles are adjustable. Number of players is selectable and a timer can be set for turns.

Tile Mechanics

  • Each tile has four edges that must match adjacent tiles.
  • Rotate the selected tile by clicking it again, or with the 'Rotate Tile' button.
  • Tiles must be placed adjacent to at least one existing tile (unless Free Play mode is enabled).
  • Streets on touching edges must line up exactly.
  • Colours on touching edges must match exactly.

Basic Scoring

Points are awarded based on the number of road connections made when placing a tile:

Points are awarded based on the number of matching edges made when placing a tile:

  • 1 connection: 1 point
  • 2 connections: 4 points
  • 3 connections: 9 points
  • 4 connections: 16 points

Starter Tile Multiplier

  • Connecting to a starter tile multiplies the connection score by ×2
  • Only applies to the base connection score, not to bonuses

Blank Sides

When blank sides are enabled, a blank edge never counts as a match and never scores.

Bonus Points

Center Pattern Tiles

Special tiles with patterns in their center provide immediate bonus points:

  • Centre Square (⬛): +20 points
  • Bonus Circle (⭕): +10 points

Placement Bonuses

  • Intersection: a tile with streets on all four sides earns +5 points
  • Board centre: placing on the centre cell earns +5 points

Path Bonuses

Players earn additional points by building a continuous road path between their own Center Square tiles and Bonus Circle tiles. Neutral starter tiles also count as circle endpoints, and streets belonging to any player can form part of the path.

  • Completing your first path earns a one-off connection bonus of +20 points
  • Each tile in the path is worth 3 points
  • Instant (In-Play) mode: whenever your longest path grows, the improvement is scored immediately
  • End of Game mode: your longest path is scored once, when the game ends

Strategy Tips

  1. Look for opportunities to create long paths between your Center Squares and Bonus Circles
  2. Use intersections strategically to maximize connection points
  3. Try to connect to starter tiles when making multiple connections
  4. Balance immediate scoring (connections) with long-term path development

Example Scoring

A tile placement might score as follows:

  1. Base connection score (3 connections = 9 points)
  2. Starter tile multiplier (×2 on the connection score, if adjacent to a starter tile)
  3. Center pattern bonus (if present, +10 or +20)
  4. Intersection bonus (if applicable, +5)
  5. Center placement bonus (if in center, +5)
  6. Path bonus (3 points × number of tiles in new longest path)

Note: Only the base connection score is affected by the starter tile multiplier. Pattern bonuses, path scores, and other special bonuses are added after multiplication.

Turn Actions

  • On your turn, you can:
    • Place a tile from your rack
    • Rotate a selected tile
    • Miss your turn if you can't make a valid move
  • After placing a tile, your rack is automatically refilled

Game End

The game ends when either:

  • The board is full
  • No player can make a valid move

The player with the highest score wins!
